# Logo

![OpenSnap Brand v1 Reference](Source/logo-v1-reference.png)

This image is the **OpenSnap Brand v1 Reference**. It is the official visual direction for the project, not yet final production logo artwork.

## Philosophy

The OpenSnap mark is based on a 60% / 40% window split.

The larger blue pane represents focus. The smaller dark pane represents context. Together they communicate the central product idea: arrange the workspace into a balanced shape that is immediately understandable.

## Why 60/40 Exists

The 60/40 split reflects the original motivation for OpenSnap:

- 60% for the primary task
- 40% for supporting context
- one gesture or shortcut
- no decision fatigue

This is more than a layout preset. It is the product philosophy in visual form.

## Shape

The mark should remain simple: two rounded vertical panes with a clear proportional relationship. Avoid extra dividers, shadows, outlines, or decorative geometry unless required for a specific app icon treatment.

## Safe Area

Use at least one pane-gap of clear space around the mark. In app icon contexts, keep the mark optically centered and avoid placing it too close to the rounded icon mask.

## Padding

For square containers, the mark should generally occupy 70% to 78% of the available width. The top and bottom padding should feel balanced with the side padding.

## Minimum Size

The mark must remain recognizable at 16x16. At small sizes, preserve the two-pane relationship before preserving gradients or shadow details.

## Monochrome

Monochrome versions should keep the split visible through contrast, spacing, or outline.

Preferred variants:

- black glyph on light backgrounds
- white glyph on dark backgrounds
- outline version for low-emphasis contexts

## Light Mode

Use the blue primary pane and dark neutral secondary pane on light surfaces. Keep shadows subtle and native.

## Dark Mode

Use brighter blue only when needed for contrast. The secondary pane may shift toward a lighter neutral so the split remains visible against dark surfaces.

## App Icon Usage

The app icon may use a soft macOS-style rounded square container. The mark should remain the hero. Avoid adding badges, arrows, lightning bolts, or window chrome to the primary icon.

## Future Evolution

Future refinements should polish geometry, radius, color, spacing, and optical balance. They should not replace the 60/40 identity.
